# Human Context: System Translation Dashboard

**Translation 1.0: Holding the System to Its Own Word**
When evaluating the K-12 public education system, we have to hold it to its own stated goals. The state claims the objective is to produce a student ready for college, career, and "civic engagement."

To meet that standard, a student must pass Math, English, and Science. Think of it like a manufacturing assembly line. If the Math station is where the highest number of units fail, then Math is the bottleneck of the entire factory. Even if a student is proficient in English, failing Math means the system failed to produce a fully standard-compliant graduate. 

Furthermore, an audit of the actual Math test reveals it does not measure the high-level strategic thinking required for true "civic engagement." It mostly tests basic procedures. So, not only is Math the biggest bottleneck, but the test itself isn't measuring the state's ultimate promise to the taxpayer.

**Translation 2.0: The Accountability Shift**
If a car factory produces a batch of vehicles with faulty brakes, a true Process Audit shuts down the assembly line to fix the machines making the brakes. 

Over the years, the education system changed how it measures success. Instead of auditing the factory, it built a "Student Reporting System." In our car factory example, this is the equivalent of printing out a sticker for the windshield that says, "This car is currently developing its braking skills," and shipping it to the dealership anyway. The state changed the label on the gauge to avoid fixing the factory floor.
