Executive Summary: Why Public K-12 Has Failed
Public K-12 education in the United States is failing to deliver basic proficiency in math and English. Despite high levels of funding and compensation, outcomes remain far below global peers. The system's shortcomings are systemic, not incidental.
Key Failures
* Accountability: Students are advanced and even graduated without demonstrating minimum proficiency. Staff compensation is largely disconnected from student outcomes.
* Focus: Math instruction is confined to a single period, while reading is reinforced across subjects. This imbalance produces consistent gaps in proficiency.
* Educator Proficiency: Many teachers lack strong math backgrounds, undermining their ability to scaffold lessons and anchor concepts in real-world relevance.
* Deflections: Excuses abound-blaming parents or disengaged students-while ignoring that many students are years behind and the system itself is failing them.
Ignored Solutions Process improvement methodologies-proven in medicine, manufacturing, the military, and even professional sports-are absent from Public K-12. Accountability measures introduced under No Child Left Behind have been resisted for decades, often due to union opposition.
Conclusion The failures of Public K-12 are not inevitable. Proven methods exist to transform performance, but they require courage to confront entrenched interests and demand accountability. Without systemic correction, the cycle of excuses will continue, and students will remain unprepared for the future.
