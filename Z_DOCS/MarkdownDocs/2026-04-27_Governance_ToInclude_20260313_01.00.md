K-12 Pipeline Forensic Audit & Architectural Governance Document
Part 1: Standard Work & Rules of Engagement
The foundational logic and processing rules established to ensure an unbiased, mathematically verifiable audit of K-12 public education.
* The Statutory Constraint (The Definition of Yield): The system's mandated objective is the production of a citizen equipped for "college, career, and civic life." The state-mandated Smarter Balanced Assessments (SBAC) act as the definitive, legal arbiter of this quality.
* The Tri-Factor Bottleneck: Success requires proficiency in Math, ELA, and Science. Because Math consistently demonstrates the highest defect rate across the "All Students" subgroup, it is treated as the system's primary constraint. A failure in Math renders the unit defective against the statutory requirement for quantitative civic engagement.
* The Fully Burdened Cost (The Denominator Rule): Pipeline costs are never calculated using year-over-year tracking, as FERPA redactions and normal geographic churn make individual unit tracking impossible. Instead, the total historical K-10 financial inputs (S-275 and expenditures) are divided strictly by the surviving 10th-grade "Expected to Test" population. The financial weight of all systemic churn and dropouts is automatically absorbed into the final per-unit cost of the surviving cohort.
* Labor Cost Normalization: To allow a direct, accurate comparison with the "average Joe's" annual salary, all K-12 labor costs are normalized against a standard 2,080-hour work year.
* The Redaction Interpolation Rule: Federal FERPA rules force the suppression of data at the extremes (e.g., <10% or >90%). This structural invisibility is addressed by using interpolated proxy values, explicitly flagged in the architecture to maintain a continuous cohort view without hallucinating missing units.
Part 2: The Translation Layer & Poka-Yoke Architecture
The structural failsafes built to prevent processing failures and ensure query accuracy across competing state ledgers.
* The Master Entity Dictionary: The architecture strictly segregates the plain-English human interface from OSPI's competing primary keys (the Financial orgCode vs. Assessment orgID). A flattened dictionary collapses OSPI's 7-column schema and assigns a single, universal LSS_UID to every physical entity.
* String Mutation Control: A forensic baseline extraction revealed over 3,700 records for approximately 2,940 physical schools due to historical naming truncations and administrative appends (e.g., "Closed after 2014"). The ETL process groups strictly by SchoolOrganizationid, retaining only the most recent nomenclature to prevent fractured cohort pipelines.
* The Charter Flag (Peer-to-Peer Benchmarking): Charter schools act as sovereign districts but are barred from local levy funds, operating with roughly 25% fewer financial inputs. Because the state codes them with the exact same CurrentSchoolType ("P" for Public) as traditional schools, naive county-wide averages will fatally mix these two distinct manufacturing lines. A hardcoded Charter_Flag in the dictionary acts as an automated tripwire, forcing the separation of lottery-funded charters from levy-funded public schools for accurate benchmarking.
Part 3: Scope Boundaries & System Defect Management
Anomalies and bureaucratic masking discovered during the initial Measurement System Analysis (MSA).
* The ESD Masquerade & Severe Defect Offloading: Analysis revealed instances where Educational Service Districts (ESDs) ceased acting as regional support centers and registered as dummy districts. These shadow districts predominantly house Type "R" (Dropout Re-engagement) and Type "J" (Juvenile Detention) facilities.
* Strict Scope Boundary (Type P Only): To prevent districts from deploying a "societal defect" defense, the baseline data extraction is strictly limited to Type P (Public) traditional and charter schools. Type R and Type J facilities are excluded from the baseline. This forces traditional districts to own their original cohorts; the cost of units lost to the juvenile or dropout systems is mathematically absorbed as yield loss within the Type P financial denominator.
* The "Frozen Ear" Revelation: Direct admission from the State Board of Education confirmed that the "college-ready" 10th-grade SBAC is effectively a mid-year Algebra I test. Forensic auditing verified that 0% of the questions measure Cognitive Level 4 (Extended Strategic Modeling). The state's accepted QA gauge is currently incapable of measuring the statistical sovereignty required for actual civic engagement.
