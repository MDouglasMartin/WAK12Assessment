Under the No Child Left Behind (NCLB) Act, states were required to establish academic achievement standards based on at least three performance levels: advanced, proficient, and basic (or partially proficient). While the specific names for these levels varied by state, Level 3 typically represented "Proficient," and Level 4 represented "Advanced," though this was not universally the case. 
Here is what the achievement levels generally represented:
* Basic/Partially Proficient: This level indicated that students had not yet reached the state's expectations for proficiency in a given subject.
* Proficient: This level, often defined as Level 3, was meant to represent a student's demonstrated competence over challenging subject matter. NCLB required all students to reach the proficient level by 2014.
* Advanced: This level, often defined as Level 4, was for students who had mastered a subject and could demonstrate superior performance.
