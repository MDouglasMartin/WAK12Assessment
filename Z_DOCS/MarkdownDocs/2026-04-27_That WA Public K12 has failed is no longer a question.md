That WA Public K12 has failed is no longer a question.  The question is "can it be fixed."
Despite teacher salaries as high as $170,000.00 / 180-day work year and administrator salaries that begin there, WA K12 has an over 70% FAILURE rate in getting students minimally proficient.  Can it be fixed? 
My belief when I approached several schools to try and do volunteer Math tutoring was that it needed to be.  What I learned having "gotten in" makes me question whether it can.  Here's what I encountered in practice and in researching data and policies:
We have an unaccountable system:  
Students are promoted from grade to grade (rewarded) despite failing to meet minimum proficiencies for the next grade. This is resulting in a some 40-60% of students arriving in HS 3 to 6 YEARS behind.  Another way to look at this is... there is a lack of quality control.
Teachers are rewarded for longevity and the classes they take and pass and not on what they produce.  Consequently, we have teachers making $170,000 /180-day work year with a less than 10% success rate in getting students minimally proficient.
