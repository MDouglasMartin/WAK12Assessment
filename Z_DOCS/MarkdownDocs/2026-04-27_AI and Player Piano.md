AI, Robotics, "a living wage" and Public K12; an Apology.
In 1952 Kurt Vonnegut Jr wrote "Player Piano", a book about "a near-future society that is almost totally mechanized eliminating the need for human laborers... [resulting in]... conflict between the wealthy upper class of engineers and managers who keep society running and the lower class whose skills and purpose in society have been replaced by machines." (Wikipedia)  
Vonnegut dystopian prediction did not anticipate AI or its potential impact on mechanization/robotics.  But we as a society had better... because we are on the cusp of revolution that will make the industrial revolution look like a burp.  How close are we?
Two years ago I had an abdominal surgery performed "robotically"... under the supervision of a surgeon. 
Farmers are using AI and automation for planting, monitoring, tending and harvesting their crops.  
We are seeing machines being used to "3D-print" houses.  
AI in combination with Robotics is being used in warfare as well as in academic research.
And the list is increasing at an incredible rate. How fast?
This morning, some highly unionized nations at the UN are advocating to mandate limiting the use of AI and robotics in industry and large business until Union members are re-trained and re-purposed.  
At the same time, we have local influencers and politicians demanding a minimum wage that establishes "a living wage for a 40 hr work week."  What happens when that wage exceeds what machines and AI can provide for half or less of the cost and with better quality? When artificial intelligence and automation meets artificial value added!  We're about to find out.
I don't know what post Gen Z generations are or will be facing in "the market."  But I'm relatively sure critical thinking will be a significant part of the requisite skills sets for success.  Math proficiency, if not a requisite to Critical Thinking is highly linked with it.  
Over the last 10 years, Washington Public K12 has an over 70% average FAILURE rate getting students to a MINIMUM level of math proficiency.   And it appears that, rather than fix the problem, OSPI and/or the Washington State Board of Education have created and are reporting out a new (lower) standard to make it appear students are performing better.  The "apples to apples" comparison of performance against prior years, however, shows at best, no change.  
In brief, if I am even marginally correct, we are not preparing over two thirds of our students to succeed in this dystopian future.  With over 37% of households in Washington already receiving public assistance... how much more can we absorb... and where exactly is the tipping point?
I don't believe we can replace teachers with AI or robots. BUT, we do need teachers (and administrators) who are held accountable for getting the job done; empowered to get the job done; and compensated (or let go) based on how well they get the job done.
Our students deserve it.  Our future depends on it.
