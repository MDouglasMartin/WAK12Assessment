1. The Core Philosophy & Framing (The "Why")
This section defines the "Citizen's Dashboard" approach, moving away from academic jargon and defining exactly what a successful product (student) looks like in this model.
* Sovereignty Defined: The objective of the K-12 system must be "Quantitative and Logical Sovereignty." This is the ability to independently audit government policy, spot statistical fallacies, and engage in civic life without relying on emotional narrative.
* The "Dual-Standard" Expose: We explicitly separate the "Graduation Standard" from the "College Ready" standard.
o The Bridge (Level 2.6 / Score 2595): A temporary 2015 policy to prevent graduation rates from crashing. It acts as a 9th-grade Algebra I floor. It does not mean the student is ready for life or college.
o College Ready (Level 3 / Score 2614): The actual threshold where a student escapes the need for remedial math.
* The Remediation Surcharge: We recognize a "Double Tax" in the LSS model. Taxpayers fund the K-12 pipeline (approx. $274,000), but 75% of students hit the "Bridge" and are forced to pay out-of-pocket for non-credit remedial math at community colleges. 
2. The Measurement Integrity Audit (The "What")
This section documents our physical audit of the QA tool (the SBAC 10th Grade Math Test) to prove it cannot measure the state's claimed objective of "civic engagement."
* SBAC Forensic Mapping (26-Item Sample Audit): 
o Levels 1 & 2 (73% of test): Purely procedural/compliance math (e.g., plugging numbers into a given formula). Useful for manual labor, but zero utility for civic policy evaluation.
o Level 3 (27% of test): Basic reasoning (e.g., refuting a claim).
o Level 4 (0% of test): Strategic modeling with multiple variables (the exact skill needed to evaluate a tax levy or climate model) was entirely absent.
* The "Science Gap" Reality: We established that higher WCAS (Science) scores do not equal STEM proficiency. The Science assessment functions largely as a Reading Comprehension (ELA) test in a lab coat, inflating the public perception of system health. 
3. The Competency Ceiling (The "Who")
This section documents the systemic feedback loop preventing the factory from producing Level 4 outputs.
* The HB 1621 Revelation: In April 2019, Washington removed the requirement for teacher candidates to pass the WEST-B basic skills test (an 8th/9th-grade math assessment). 
* The LSS Verdict: A manufacturing line staffed by operators who are not required to pass a 9th-grade proficiency floor cannot systematically produce 10th-graders capable of high-level civic logic. The system is prioritized for labor throughput, not product proficiency. 
4. The "Wrong Paths" (What Was Rejected & Why)
Documenting these rejected paths ensures an AI doesn't try to revert to standard OSPI reporting norms.
* Rejected Path: Using "Scale Scores" to educate the public.
o What we tried: Explaining the difference between a 2595 and a 2614.
o Why we killed it: Scale scores are statistical white noise to the average citizen. 
o The Fix: We map the scores to Functional Utility (Life, Career, Civic Participation) and the dollar cost of the Remediation Tax. 
* Rejected Path: Leading the public dashboard with Demographic/Equity narratives.
o What we tried: Breaking down the data strictly by race, gender, or income. 
o Why we killed it: It triggers partisan defenses and allows the system to blame poverty for its failures. 
o The Fix: We focus strictly on the "Unit Cost of a Sovereign Citizen," which affects all demographics equally. Inefficiency is the ultimate inequity.
