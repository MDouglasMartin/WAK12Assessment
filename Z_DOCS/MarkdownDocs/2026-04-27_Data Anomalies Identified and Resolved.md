Data Anomalies Identified and Resolved - Assessment Data 2014-15 to 2024-25
Twin Harbors / New Market Skills Center (SchoolCode 5208, Aberdeen SD, Grays Harbor County, 2015-16) 44 records have a CSV parsing error caused by a comma embedded in the school name "Twin Harbors, A Branch of New Market Skills Center". The unquoted comma caused all columns to shift right by one position in the source file, placing the school name fragment in SchoolOrganizationId and student group names in GradeLevel. All 44 records are suppressed (N<10), school type is V (vocational), and none pass the CurrentSchoolType = 'p' filter. Zero impact on the fact table. Handled by TRY_CAST in _Out view.
GradeLevel column in Assessment_14-15_to_21-22 The source table stores GradeLevel as varchar and contains 'All Grades' (1.9M rows - all-grades rollup rows), 'KG' (100K rows), and student group names in 44 rows (the Twin Harbors parsing error above). All normalized to NULL or 99 in the _IN view and filtered by the WHERE clause before reaching the fact table. TRY_CAST in _Out provides the safety net.
OSPI Column Renaming Post-Covid (2024-25 vintage) Following the 2020-21 reporting year where grade 10/11 math failure rates exceeded 85%, OSPI renamed three columns while retaining identical calculation methodology. CountMetStandard ? Count Consistent Grade Level Knowledge And Above, PercentMetStandard ? Percent Consistent Grade Level Knowledge And Above, PercentMetTestedOnly ? Percent Consistent Tested Only. These are the same metrics. The rename is documented in the ztt load comments and normalized to standard column names in the staging table.
GradeLevel = 9 present in data Grade 9 SBAC math records exist in the dataset, concentrated in 2020-21 and 2021-22. Likely COVID cohort catch-up testing for students who missed grade 8 assessment in the cancelled 2019-20 testing year. Retained in the fact table pending confirmation of year distribution
